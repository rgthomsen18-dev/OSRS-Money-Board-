Item-set arbitrage. Buy the four pieces, exchange them for the packed set via a GE clerk, sell the set — or the reverse. Both directions priced live with the 2% sell tax on whichever side you sell.

Margins are per set and ignore buy limits — most set components have small limits, so treat this as opportunity-spotting, not bulk throughput. "Pack" buys pieces (at ask) and sells the set (taxed); "unpack" buys the set and sells pieces (taxed). Only fully-repaired Barrows pieces are tradeable.

Flip finder. Ranks tradeable items by realistic flipping profit from live GE data. Margin is the spread after the 2% sell tax (buy at the low, sell at the high); Limit is how many you can buy per 4-hour window; Profit / 4h is margin × the buy limit — the hard cap on how many you can purchase per 4-hour window, which varies by item. That cap is why a day’s profit is far below “margin × unlimited”: at most you clear it six times a day, and usually less across a basket of items. Liquidity is separate — a fill? tag flags items that trade too thinly to realistically fill their limit in a window. Raise Min volume to keep offers filling fast and to cut thinly-traded noise. Tap any item for buy/sell, ROI, volume, alch value, and its ~90-day price trend. Top 150 shown.
